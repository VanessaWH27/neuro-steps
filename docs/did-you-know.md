# Did You Know?

Interesting facts about the brain, neuroscience, and Alzheimer's Disease research.

## About the Brain

- The human brain weighs approximately **3 pounds (1.4 kg)** but uses about **20% of the body's energy**.
- The brain contains approximately **86 billion neurons**.
- Information in the brain can travel at speeds up to **268 miles per hour**.

## About Memory

- The brain can store an estimated **2.5 petabytes of information** (equivalent to about 3 million hours of TV shows).
- Short-term memory can hold about **7 items** for no more than **20-30 seconds**.
- **Sleep** plays a crucial role in consolidating memories.

## About Alzheimer's Disease

- An estimated **50 million people worldwide** are living with dementia today.
- In the U.S., of the 5.5 million people age 65 living with Alzheimer's disease, **two-thirds are women**.
- Alzheimer's disease is the **most common cause of dementia**, accounting for **60-80% of cases**.
- The disease was first described by **Dr. Alois Alzheimer in 1906**.
- Brain changes associated with Alzheimer's may begin **20 years or more before symptoms appear**.

## About Research

- The **first FDA-approved treatment** for Alzheimer's was released in **1993**.
- Modern brain imaging techniques can detect brain changes **years before symptoms develop**.
- **Exercise and a healthy diet** may help reduce the risk of cognitive decline.
